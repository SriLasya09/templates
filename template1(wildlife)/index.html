The lower the better

When taking pictures of wildlife it is important to be as close to the ground as possible. An eye to eye angle will give the image a much more dramatic impact and will give the right perspective, helping to show the dimension of the animal. The Swahili word for “zebra” is “pundamilia,” which translates literally to “striped donkey.” The function of a zebra’s stripes is still up for debate, though it is widely thought that the stripes act as a way of confusing predators, either by camouflaging or making the zebra’s form ambiguous from a distance.
